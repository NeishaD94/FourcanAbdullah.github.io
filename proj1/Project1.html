Things to do in IceLand

The aurora Borealis takes its name from the Roman goddess of the dawn, Aurora, and the Greekname for the north wind Borealis From September to April, Iceland is a hotspot for this magnificaient lightshow. The Aurora Borealis, more commonly known reffered to as the Northern lightshow is a natural Phenomenon created when particles emmited by the sun interact in the atmosphere in the Earths magnetic field. This releases energy, causing peculiur lumminous green streaks across the sky.

Ice climbing & hiking on glaciers When making a trip to Iceland, it is hard not to pay special attention to the country's namesake—namely, its 4,500 square miles of glacier. Ice climbing on the glaciers is practiced year-round and takes place mainly on the Sólheimajökull and Svínafellsjökull glaciers in the south of Iceland, to which day trips are offered from Reykjavík (and Skaftafell).
